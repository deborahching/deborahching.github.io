John Doughnuts

This is a project for a doughnuts start-up company, John Doughnuts, in Copenhagen. Their company tenet is bringing joy to the office and sustainable. Among this project, a brand identity was created.

Logo Design

With the company tent, bring joy to others, an enjoy facial expression and the doughnuts was the key elements of the logo, presenting the joyful concept. 

Brand Pattern

Brand pattern was created by different flavours of the doughnuts. Based on the colours of the ingredients, the colours of the brand was more organic and natural, echos with another company tent, creating more healthy snacks.

Packaging Design

Inside the packaging, under the cover, an empty space allow customer to leave their words to their colleagues. At the bottom, some fun facts of the doughnuts ingredients will be shown.

Social Media Strategy

Delivery Van Design

Another service the company aimed to provide is the delivery service, as they are a startup company, promotion of brand is important, a delivery van was also designed to further advertise the company.

Billboard Design For Greenpeace

This design aiming at raising the awareness of public on the global warming issue. Making use of the transparent billboard design, pedestrians from the outside are allow to look into the city through the billboard, that are affected by the global warming issue, flooded or air polluted, which is giving the feeling of foreseeing the future through the billboard. At the same time, people waiting in the bus stop could also gain a feeling of being trapped in the climate issue.

The design was designed to place outdoor and at different places, to bring out the idea of a global issue.

MBTI EDUCATION

This is a work that proposed the future education system (i.e. personalised teaching), MBTI types are used for this educating method. In this work, I have been using two distinct personalities, INFP and ESTJ, as example to show how does the education system work in classroom. The target audience of the design is the general public who are curious about the future education.

Concept Design

16 characters designed based on the 16 MBTI types. Characters are designed more childlike and colourful. While the difference of characters is showing the uniqueness and potentials of children.

INFP Lesson Prototype Video

Stop motion video by LEGOs to prototype the lesson setup of INFP type. The video proposed 3 main ideas for INFP students: individual work, visualised materials, and personalised lesson materials.

ESTJ Lesson Prototype Video

Stop motion video by LEGOs to prototype the lesson setup of ESTJ type. The video proposed 3 main ideas for ESTJ students: group work, logical materials, and interactive class activity.

Medicine Project

Taking medicine by mistake is commonly found among older patients in Hong Kong. Some may lead to fatalities, particularly for older patients who live in solitude. With no doctors, pharmacists or friends on hand to help, these elderly patients have to struggle with the small print and poor information design often found on medicine labels or medicine packaging. This project is a group work and contains two parts: medicine label redesign and experimental medication storage design.

Medicine Label Redesign

Medicine labelling and packaging should not be seen as simply as a sticker and a place showing information. It should be reconsidered as an intrinsic part of the user-centred design that ought to be usable, understandable and accessible for all users, particularly for elderly. We tried to recreate a more user-friendly label design.

Pictogram Design

Pictograms are designed based on users' needs, which indicate the time, remains simple, and with supporting words.

Experimental Medication Storage Design


In this part, we tried to create a new mechanism for older people to have a better medicine-taking experience. As observed, elderlies usually take medicine in the morning and at night, which the design are divided into two sides for the two time slots. In the design, we also created different "heads" that allows user to customised base on their needs, such as toothbrush.  
There is three key words for the design: HABIT / CUSTOMISED / PORTABLE

Eco-House

Climate change, has been greatly intensify in recent years, the issue had been threatening the living of human beings and especially our next generation. This project is a collaboration with the Rentemestervej's Library, to raise the awareness of the public on the issue. THE ECO-HOUSE OF 2050 is the concept created, which is a modern house filled with recycled and sophisticated furniture. During the exhibition 5 posters that represents eco-house’s artefacts, and 5 audio sounds and descriptions to immerge the spectator in the eco-house will be displayed. 

The poster is showcasing what recycled materials are used to create the artefacts. By seeing the artefacts through the materials they are built from, a clear suggestion could be give to audience and allow them to use their own imagination on this proposed future.

Exhibition setup

The exhibition is planned to simulate a home feeling, audience are requires to walk through "doors" to see different artefacts which to feel like walking from rooms to rooms at home.
